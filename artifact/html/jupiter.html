WATER: There are tiny amounts of water in the atmosphere, but not on Jupiter itself!

OXYGEN: A tiny tiiiiiiny bit, less than 1% and definitely not enough for us to breathe.

ATMOSPHERE: Jupiter's atmosphere is made mostly of hydrogen and helium (what we use for balloons!).

GRAVITY: Because Jupiter is so huge, it has a huge gravitational pull! This means you would weigh more than twice as much on Jupiter as you would on Earth.

MOON: Did you know that Jupiter has 4 large moons and a ton of tiny captured asteroids? Added together, that's 67 moons (and counting)!

welcome to the gas giants!

Jupiter is huuuuuuuuuge. Do you know how many Earths can fit into Jupiter? Around 1,323. It's also the fifth closest planet to the Sun, and has the shortest day out of all of the planets!

Jupiter is a "gas giant." This means it's mostly made of gas, and doesn't have a solid surface. This is the opposite of Earth and other planets closer to the sun, which are made of mostly rock.
